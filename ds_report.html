Crypto Data Science Assignment Report

Prepared for the Hyperliquid / Fear-and-Greed trading analysis assignment.

1. Task Summary

The assignment asks for an analysis of the relationship between trader behavior on Hyperliquid and Bitcoin market sentiment, using a historical execution dataset and the daily Fear and Greed Index. The core goal is to identify how sentiment regimes affect performance, execution style, and risk, and then use those findings to build a leakage-safe predictive model.

2. Data Overview

Historical Trader Data

  • 211,224 rows, 32 unique accounts, 246 unique coins.
  • Main fields: account, coin, execution price, size, side, timestamp IST, start position, direction, closed PnL, crossed, fee, trade ID.
  • Data is heavy-tailed, with large outliers in Closed PnL and Size USD.

Fear and Greed Index

  • 2,644 daily rows spanning 2018-02-01 to 2025-05-02.
  • Fields: timestamp, value, classification, date.
  • Classification buckets: Extreme Fear, Fear, Neutral, Greed, Extreme Greed.

3. Preprocessing and Join Logic

The most important technical step is the timestamp alignment. The trade data is in IST, while sentiment is daily and indexed by date. To avoid leakage and off-by-one-day mismatches, the pipeline converts trade timestamps from IST to UTC and then joins on the UTC date key.
  1. Parse Timestamp IST as DD-MM-YYYY HH:MM.
  2. Subtract 5 hours 30 minutes to obtain UTC time.
  3. Derive date_utc and join to the sentiment date field.
  4. Winsorize Closed PnL and Size USD at the 1st and 99th percentiles for robust statistics.
  5. Remove spot dust conversion rows and similar administrative noise from analysis tables.

4. Exploratory Findings

  • Performance differs materially across sentiment regimes.
  • Maker/taker behavior shifts with fear and greed; traders become more aggressive in greed regimes.
  • Trade sizing and leverage proxies increase in higher sentiment regimes, indicating stronger risk-taking.
  • Account performance is heterogeneous: a few accounts dominate total realized PnL.

6. Predictive Modeling Pipeline

The modeling notebooks build a leakage-safe classifier for Closed PnL > 0 using lagged and rolling features only. The validation strategy is walk-forward with purging and embargo. The champion model is a boosted-tree baseline combined with thresholding, confidence-based sizing, and a final guard gate.

  • Primary metric set: ROC-AUC, PR-AUC, F1, Brier score.
  • Trading metric set: total PnL, max drawdown, hit rate, profit factor.
  • Thresholds and confidence sizing are optimized out-of-sample on walk-forward predictions.

The final modeling design also uses the strongest practical ideas from the advanced modeling notes: calibrated probabilities for position sizing, lagged sentiment velocity, account momentum features, and purged time-series evaluation. Triple-barrier labeling and full graph-based wash-trading loops are documented as future extensions because the available fill-level dataset does not contain a clean future path or wallet-transfer graph.
